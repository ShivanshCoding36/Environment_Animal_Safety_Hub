The Rainbow Graveyard

Coral reefs are the rainforests of the sea. They're dying, and taking 25% of all marine life with them.

Clownfish

Lost their anemone homes

The iconic Nemo now struggles to find protection. Bleached corals mean no anemones, which means no home for clownfish families.

Habitat Lost: 50%

Sea Stars

Wasting away in warm waters

Sea star wasting disease spreads rapidly in warmer oceans. Entire populations disintegrate within days.

Population: -90%

Cleaner Shrimp

No fish to clean

Their entire purpose disappears as fish populations decline. The intricate cleaning stations sit empty.

Reef Cover: -80%

Coral bleaching isn't just about color loss. It's about the silent collapse of entire cities of marine life. Each bleached coral represents thousands of homeless creatures.

Honoring the Grief

Feeling grief for animals and ecosystems is healthy. It means you care deeply. Here's how to process that grief:

Validate Your Feelings

Your grief is real. It's okay to mourn for species, habitats, and the future they represent.

Find Community

Connect with others who understand this specific type of grief. You're not alone.

Channel into Action

Grief can fuel meaningful action. Every positive choice matters.

Practice Eco-Mindfulness

Spend time in nature, even small patches. Remember what you're fighting for.
